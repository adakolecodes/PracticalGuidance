Residence Attacks
Threat Pattern Index
KFR is a southern and inland corridor threat — not confined to one state. Road movement is the primary vulnerability.
Perpetrators include criminal gangs, organised kidnap rings, and politically-linked groups exploiting weak policing and economic pressures.
Attackers exploit choke points, fake incidents, and poor emergency response windows along known routes.

Urban access risk meets industrial-site exposure
Asaba

Ebedei

Local drivers

PEL implication: Predictable commute windows and rural approach roads elevate exposure. Secure routes must match secure site perimeters.
Regional Snapshot
Lagos
Complex urban threat with surveillance and follow-home risk
Dense Urban Threat
Petty crime and targeted kidnaps overlap. Underlit highways and isolated estates are the primary exposure zones.
Surveillance & Follow-Home
Victims watched from workplace, fuel stops, or social venues — then targeted near their residence.
Traffic Ambushes
Congestion exploited for grab-and-run incidents. Fake checkpoints used to stop and divert vehicles.
Protection Principle
Route discipline + low visibility. Never disclose home location or commute timing in public forums.
Regional Snapshot
Ondo: Ore Corridor
Transit security matters more than destination security
Night Movement
Visibility drops, response time lengthens, and attackers can isolate vehicles with far less risk.
Secondary Roads
Diversions onto less-controlled roads remove assistance options and create ambush conditions.
Unplanned Stops
Any uncontrolled stop creates a surveillance and ambush window. Pre-clear all stops before departure.
Long Transit Windows
Extended journeys increase predictability and fatigue — both exploitable by attackers.
The Ore corridor has a documented history of highway robbery and kidnap — especially after dark and on less-controlled stretches. Treat every transit as an active security event.

Personal Security
Behaviour & Situational Awareness
Do This
Low profile. No visible wealth, branded gear, or electronics on display.
Vary routes and routines wherever practical.
Security by default: locked doors, secured windows, active exterior lighting.
Watch For
Same vehicle or motorcycle seen repeatedly near your route or home.
Strangers asking about your title, shift times, or family routines.
Loitering near gates, parking areas, or site approach roads.
Movement Security
Travel & Movement Controls
Good journey management reduces attacker opportunity
Control Pillar Weighting
Daylight only on high-risk routes.
Vetted drivers and company-approved vehicles only.
Check-in every 30–60 min on long transits.
Pre-cleared stops only — no isolated rest stops.
Vehicle Security
Vehicle & Convoy Measures
Small habits preserve options in a crisis
Vehicle Readiness
Current maintenance, fuel margin, charged phone, power bank, water, and basic emergency kit.
Traffic Posture
Doors locked, windows controlled, enough following distance to manoeuvre away from a threat.
Escort Discipline
Follow escort lead. Maintain comms discipline. Do not improvise route or stop decisions.
Suspected Checkpoint
Slow, assess, call Security Focal Person. Never exit the vehicle until identity is verified.
Facility Security
Workplace & Facility Security
The approach road and gate routine matter as much as the site
Access Control
Controlled access, visitor verification, and perimeter patrols at Ebedei and office locations.
Staggered Shifts & Sign-in Logs
Staff sign-in and sign-out logs. Stagger shift changes to reduce predictable clustering.
Lighting & Parking Control
External lighting, parking supervision, and gate controls for facilities and staff accommodation.
A strong perimeter is undermined by insecure commuting patterns and informal access arrangements.
Controlled Perimeter
Residential Security
Home & Residence Safety
Residences are often the softest target
Harden the Residence
Secure doors, perimeter fencing, exterior lighting, and CCTV where feasible.
Digital Discipline
No live location, travel schedules, or residence photos shared online or in public groups.
Vetted Domestic Staff
Validate all domestic staff and casual workers. Insider knowledge is a primary enabler of home attack.
Community Network
Emergency pickup points, gate verification habits, and a trusted-neighbour contact network.

Crisis Response
If Threatened or Abducted
1
Prioritise Survival
Comply with demands where resistance would increase violence risk. Compliance is not defeat.
2
Observe & Retain
Memorise vehicle descriptions, voices, direction, and notable stops — without provoking attackers.
3
Activate Alert
Silent alert or contact with Security Focal Person — only when safe to do so.
4
After Release
Medical attention immediately. Preserve all evidence. Report via company channels.
Incident Management
Incident Response & Reporting
Fast reporting improves coordination and support
Contact
Notify PEL Security Focal Person, local police, and company Incident Manager immediately.
Coordinate
No independent negotiation. All comms through designated Incident Manager.
Preserve
Keep all messages, call logs, and communications intact for security and law-enforcement use.
Support
PEL coordinates authority liaison, family comms, medical care, and psychosocial support.
Independent media engagement or third-party negotiation without company authorisation can endanger the hostage and undermine law-enforcement coordination.
