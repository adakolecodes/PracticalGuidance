# SLIDE 4

## Regional snapshot: Delta (Asaba and Ebedei)

**Urban access risk meets industrial-site exposure**

### Asaba and Ebedei risk picture

* Asaba is an urban environment with greater exposure around transit hubs, informal routes, and after-hours movements.
* Ebedei carries industrial and plant-related exposure, including attacks on commuting staff, perimeter incursions, and opportunistic roadside snatching on secluded stretches.
* Local drivers include gang activity, economic incentives, poor lighting, weak road condition, and inconsistent visible security presence.

### PEL-specific implication

* Site commuting and staff transport patterns may be easier to observe than city-only office movements.
* Rural approach roads and predictable arrival windows can increase exposure if not actively managed.
* Security measures at the facility must be matched by secure approach routes, disciplined check-ins, and low-profile staff behaviour.

# SLIDE 5

## Regional snapshot: Lagos

**Complex urban threat with surveillance and follow-home risk**

* Lagos presents a dense urban threat environment where petty crime and occasional targeted kidnaps can overlap.
* Higher-risk settings include underlit highways, isolated estates, poorly controlled pickup points, and late-night commutes.
* Common vectors include traffic-congestion ambushes, fake checkpoints, opportunistic follow-home attacks, and grab-and-run incidents that can escalate into short-term captivity.
* The main protection principle in Lagos is route discipline, low visibility, and avoiding routines that expose residential location or movement timing.

# SLIDE 7

## Typical kidnap modalities

**How attacks are most likely to present**

*Steps:*

**Roadside ambush**
Attackers intercept corporate or private vehicles at choke points or quiet segments.

**Follow and grab**
Victim is watched from work, worship, social venue, fuel stop, or airport transfer and taken later.

**Home invasion**
Residence is targeted using gate deception, poor lighting, or knowledge of the occupant routine.

**Deception**
Fake roadblocks, impersonation of security forces, and staged accidents are used to stop movement.

**Snatch escalation**
A robbery or opportunistic snatch develops into forced movement and ransom demand.

# SLIDE 8

## Personal behaviour and situational awareness

**Personal security discipline lowers exposure**

* Maintain a low profile: avoid public display of wealth, branded gear, cash, or visible electronics.
* Vary routines and routes wherever practical; attackers thrive on predictability.
* Use security by default: lock doors, secure windows, and keep exterior lighting active when arriving or leaving.
* Stay alert in public spaces and reduce distraction from phones or headphones while moving.

**Watch for**
* The same motorcycle, tricycle, or vehicle appearing repeatedly near your route, home, or workplace.
* Strangers asking about your title, family, shift time, route, or who is staying in your residence.
* Unexpected route changes, meeting relocations, or requests to stop in quiet places.
* Loitering near gates, pickup points, site approaches, or poorly lit parking areas.

**Good journey management reduces opportunity for attackers**

*Chart:*

|                       | Control pillars |
|-----------------------|-----------------|
| Daylight travel | 30 |
| Vetted transport | 25 |
| Check-in protocol | 25 |

* Travel in daylight whenever possible and avoid movement after dusk on high-risk routes.
* Use vetted drivers and company-approved vehicles only for work-related movement.
* Share travel plans and live ETAs with the Security Focal * Person and use check-in intervals of 30 to 60 minutes on long transits.
* Avoid isolated rest stops; use secure service stations, controlled compounds, or pre-cleared safe locations.

| Measure | Practical expectation |
|---------|-----------------------|

# SLIDE 11

## Workplace and facility security

**The approach road and gate routine matter as much as the site**

* Enforce controlled access, visitor verification, and perimeter patrols at the Ebedei site and office locations.
* Maintain staff sign-in and sign-out logs, and stagger shift changes where practical to reduce predictable clustering.
* Strengthen parking control, external lighting, and gate supervision for facilities and staff accommodation.
* Run regular security briefings and simple lockdown or evacuation drills so staff know what to do under pressure.

**Facility takeaway**
A strong perimeter can still be undermined by insecure commuting patterns, informal access arrangements, or poor visitor control.

# SLIDE 12

## Home and residence safety

**Residences often become the softest target**

* Harden residences with secure doors, perimeter fencing, exterior lighting, and CCTV where feasible.
* Do not overshare sensitive information online such as live location, travel schedules, family routines, or residence photos.
* Use vetted domestic staff and validate new hires or casual workers through security checks where possible.
* Establish emergency pickup points, gate verification habits, and a small trusted-neighbour contact network.

---

# SLIDE 13

## What to do if threatened or abducted

**Personal survival comes first**

* If confronted, prioritise survival and comply with demands where resistance would increase violence risk.
* Memorise useful details discreetly, such as vehicle description, voices, direction of travel, or notable stops, without provoking attackers.
* Activate personal distress procedures only when safe to do so, including silent alert or contact with the Security Focal Person.
* After release or escape, seek medical attention, preserve evidence, and report immediately through company channels.

---

# SLIDE 14

## Incident response and reporting

**Fast reporting improves coordination and support**

* **Contact**: Notify the PEL Security Focal Person, local police, and the company Incident Manager immediately.

* **Coordinate**: Do not negotiate independently or engage third-party negotiators outside company direction.

* **Preserve**: Keep messages, call logs, and other communications intact for security and law-enforcement use.

* **Support**: PEL should coordinate authority liaison, family communication, medical care, and psychosocial support.
